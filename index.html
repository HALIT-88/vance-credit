The math nobody shows you
A lower credit score doesn't just feel bad.
It costs you real money. Every month.

Every loan you take out — car, home, business — comes with an interest rate directly tied to your credit score. The lower the score, the higher the rate. The higher the rate, the more you pay every single month for years. The difference between a 541 and a 680 on a $30,000 car loan can cost you over $10,000 across the life of the loan.

Vance Credit costs $97 a month. That's the math. If inaccurate items on your report are holding your score back, the cost of not addressing them is almost always greater than the cost of addressing them.

What we dispute

We tell you exactly what we can — and can't — do.

No credit repair company can legally remove accurate, verified information. Any company that tells you otherwise is violating federal law.

We dispute these
✓
Medical collections
Especially under $500 — bureaus have voluntarily agreed to remove. Also: inaccurate amounts, wrong dates, already paid.
✓
Late payments reported in error
Wrong date, wrong account, reported after you paid — all disputable.
✓
Accounts that are not yours
Identity errors, mixed credit files, fraudulent accounts.
✓
Charge-offs with wrong information
Incorrect balance, wrong date, wrong creditor — each is a disputable inaccuracy.
✓
Duplicate accounts
Same debt reported more than once — a direct FCRA violation.
✓
Items past the 7-year reporting window
Negative items have a legal reporting limit. Items still showing after that limit must be removed.
We cannot dispute these
✕
Accurate, verified negative information
If a late payment happened and is correctly reported, it cannot be removed. Period.
✕
Bankruptcies within the reporting window
If the bankruptcy occurred and is within its legal timeframe, it stays.
✕
Accurate judgments and liens
Verified court records that are accurately reported cannot be removed through disputes.
We will never dispute accurate information
Filing false disputes is illegal under the FCRA and CROA. If we look at your file and don't see enough to dispute, we will tell you before you enroll in anything.

FAQ

Questions we get every day.

Do you charge before you do any work?

Never. Federal law under CROA prohibits it — and we've built our entire operation around this fact. Your first charge fires after your first dispute round is submitted and visible in your portal. Not at signup. After the work.

Can you guarantee my score will go up?

No — and any company that tells you they can is violating federal law. We guarantee what we control: our documented effort, our dispute submissions, our follow-up. How credit bureaus respond is outside our control and outside what any legitimate company can promise.

What is the 6-Month Service Guarantee?

If after 6 months of active enrollment we have not submitted documented dispute activity on your credit file, we refund every service fee and work your file for another 6 months at no charge. This guarantees our effort and documented submissions — not specific outcomes.

How long does credit repair take?

Bureaus have 30 days by law to investigate each dispute. How long your journey takes depends on your specific file, the number of disputable items, and how bureaus respond. After reviewing your Vance Score Report, we give you an honest estimate based on what's actually in your file.

Can I do this myself for free?

Yes — and we are legally required to tell you this, so we will. You have the right to dispute inaccurate items directly with the credit bureaus at no cost. That right is explained in the Consumer Credit File Rights disclosure you receive before signing anything with us.

What's a goal track?

Auto Approved, Home Approved, or Business Funded. Different lenders look at different items. We build your dispute strategy around your specific loan type — not a one-size-fits-all approach.

Can I cancel?

Yes. Within 3 business days of signing — full refund, no questions. That's your right under federal law and it's in every Vance Credit contract. After that, cancel any time. You only pay for completed dispute rounds. No cancellation fees.

Do you work in every state?

Most U.S. states, yes. Georgia is the exception — Georgia law makes for-profit credit repair a misdemeanor. Georgia residents are routed to our partner nonprofit foundation at no charge.

Federal law requires Vance Credit to provide you with this disclosure before any contract is signed. We provide it as a separate signed document at contract execution.
